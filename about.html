Curatorial Statement

The concept behind "The Yellow Portfolio" stemmed from an image I had in mind long before I even brainstormed this project. There were times I indeed imagined myself peeking out from a box, looking out to the world far ahead.

In this exhibit, that box is conventionalism. These works of mine that I have curated are essentially my acts of practice that when put together in a certain order, form a path that gradually leads to the idea of creative liberation. Hence, I designed the exhibit's structure around that narrative.

Most of the creative works I have done so far have featured atmospheric, genre-defying worlds that blend the surreal with a grounded reality. To accommodate my ever-expanding imagination that I have nurtured over the years, I express those worlds across several mediums; photography, illustrations, and film. I have always seen mediums as interchangeable instruments that when done right, can serve a single, cohesive voice across works.

Statement

With that said, I did not create this exhibition with validation in mind, where in such case I would showing-off what I created for the sake of rigid aesthetic consistency and academic completion. Rather, my intent is to demonstrate what I can do with my existing works — re-contextualizing them and making a thematic narrative from them, as well as the strategic creative versatility of one person, as both a multimedia student and an aspiring writer-director.
