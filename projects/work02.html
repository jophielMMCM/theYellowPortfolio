AESTHETIC STALKING

2024

Photography - narrative

A series of photographs originally made for a group project that ended up with a magazine. But on my end, I was deeply immersed in creating the narrative behind the photographs as I took them on a borrowed camera.

Conceptually and story-wise, this is currently my darkest work to date; I wove a psychological thriller from the perpetrator's eyes — the titular stalker. Inspired by the infamous jaundiced color grading of modern films, most of the photos are set to a similar palette to evoke a sickly, boiling feeling that emotionally reflected our unseen stalker's psyche and surroundings. Yet, the palette proved to be highly adaptable as a "cinematic" anchor, as seen in the covers used for the magazine: the last two images in the mini-gallery provided.

This project serves as a key example of my creative identity, proving how an overarching mood can anchor a narrative across completely different formats.
